**Research statement**
My research focuses on the intersection of semantic technologies, Linked Open Data, and scientific knowledge infrastructures. I have dedicated my career to developing tools and workflows that transform raw data into structured, machine-readable knowledge, specifically through the use of Wikidata and Scholia. My work spans from early neuroimaging meta-analysis and database development to modern advancements in semantic representation and wiki-integrated workflows for sustainability transitions. I aim to bridge the gap between massive datasets and human-readable scientific insights through automated labeling and semantic connectivity.

**Personal context**
I hold a permanent research position at the Technical University of Denmark, where I balance high-level research development with academic responsibilities. My role involves the continuous evolution of digital research infrastructures and the integration of open-source technologies into scientific workflows. I am primarily focused on the long-term sustainability of semantic data structures and the development of software that facilitates open science.

**Contributions to the research community**
I am a strong advocate for Open Science and the development of research infrastructures. My primary contributions include:
*   **Infrastructure Development:** Leading the development of Scholia and WikiCite to improve the visibility and connectivity of scientific citations and bibliographic data.
*   **Semantic Tools:** Creating software and methodologies for semantic representation and the visualization of Linked Open Data (e.g., Synia).
*   **Knowledge Integration:** Developing wiki-integrated workflows to support living reviews and the automation of anatomical labeling in neuroimaging databases.
*   **Data Openness:** Promoting the use of Wikidata as a platform for editable, linked, and transparent scientific data.

**Contributions to the wider society**
My work contributes to the democratization of knowledge through the development of open-source tools that make complex scientific data accessible to the general public. By integrating scientific data with platforms like Wikidata, I facilitate greater transparency and knowledge sharing between the scientific community and societal actors, supporting global efforts in data-driven decision-making and open information access.
